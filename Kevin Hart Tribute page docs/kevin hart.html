Kevin Hart Is Inspirational

Background

Kevin Darnell Hart was born July 6, 1979 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. Kevin was raised in a single parent household by his mother Nancy Hart. His father was a cocaine addict that was in and out of jail when Kevin was growing up. Kevin used humor as a way to deal with his troubled family life. After graduating high school, Kevin attended community college of Philadelphia, but he soon realized college was not the path he wanted to take and dropped out. Later he moved to Brockton, Massachusetts finding work as a shoe salesman, pursuing a career in stand up comedy on the side. Kevin Hart's first stand up was at the Laff House in Philadelphia, which was a disaster. Kevin did not start out like the Kevin we know today; infact, in the beginning he was booed off the stage several times, also having a piece of chicken thrown at him. Kevin struggled to find his own personal style and rythm, often imitating other comedians. However, after preforming in many comdey competitions throughtout Massachusetts he imporved.

Success

Kevin Hart's comedy tours started in 2009 with I'm a Grown Little Man. Success continued in 2010 with Seriously Funny, 2011's Laugh at My Pain and 2013's Let Me Explain. Laugh at My Pain and Let Me Explain was released in the movie theaters, and Laugh at My Pain earned over $15 Million making it one of the years top selling comedy tours. In 2015, Kevin created the biggest comedy tour in history called What Now preforming in front of more than 50,000 people at Philadelphia's Lincoln Financial Field.
